Instructions for using dining utensils

Section II - The Spoon

    1. Open hand
    2. Put palm over handle
    3. Close hand
    4. Lift spoon
    5. Dip spoon into food
    6. Lift spoon
    7. Open mouth
    8. Insert spoon LARGE END FIRST
    9. Close mouth
    10. Remove spoon - DO NOT OPEN MOUTH until spoon is fully removed


FAQ Section

Q: "I lifted the spoon out of the food, but it won't go into my mouth."

A: Make sure your mouth is open BEFORE inserting the spoon.

Q: "I got the spoon into my mouth without a problem, but I only got a tiny
    bit of food."

A: You closed your hand over the big end of the spoon instead of the little
   end.

Q: "I got my hand on the spoon and was able to lift it, but I can't pick up
    the food."

A: Make sure the the spoon right-side up.

Q: "How do I tell?"

A: If your reflection is larger than normal when you look at the spoon,
   the spoon is upside down. If your reflection is upside down, the spoon
   is right-side up.

Q: "Upside down is right-side up? This is too confusing. I give up!"

A: Throw tantrum or see sub-section 4.
