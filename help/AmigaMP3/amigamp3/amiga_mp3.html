MP3 on the AMIGA

Offline version

I was tired of seeing all those web pages explaining how to get software for the PeeCee...Mac..Linux...
But I never saw a page on how to deal with MP3 on the AMIGA, so here it is!! I hope you enjoy!!
The AMIGA RULES dudes!! :))

Yes, you can hear MP3 sound files on your AMIGA! Of course you need a faaast CPU... a 060 is prefered ;) But it depends of course of the MP3 quality , some are more CPU-demanding, some are less (yeah!) :)

[ The players | The encoders | Links to MP3 sites | Configuring your browser to play MP3 files ]

  • The players

    • SongPlayer

      The BEST MP3 player for the AMIGA, just go and get it! It plays several audio formats, has a MUI GUI (also works without MUI with CLI), has karaoke support, and it's fast. Don't think twice, just get it! :)

    • MPEGAPlayer (for DeliTracker v2.27)

      This is was my old setup before SongPlayer appeared, it's fast, easy to install, and has a nice GUI (Graphical User Interface).
      I have an A1260/50 Mhz 18 RAM, so my config is like MPEGA's default, I just and enabled Force mono, because multitasking gets smoother and because I have my AMIGA connected to my Seymour Duncan guitar amplifier :) (yes! I play guitar... check my Home Page! :)
      Don't forget that MPEGAPlayer requires DeliTracker!

    • MPEGA (no GUI)

      From the same author of MPEGAPlayer (Stéphane Tavenard), this is the CLI-based version of his MPEG audio player, it allows, amongst other things, the saving of MP3 files into AIFF files, for you to play later (handy for you slow CPU users :). Play16 v1.7 plays very well AIFF and other sound files and does 14-bit output.

  • The encoders

    • mp3enc

      The only real mp3 encoder for the AMIGA, it's still a bit alpha stage, but it manages to encode 44 Khz Stereo 16 bit audio files :)
      And it has optimized versions for several CPUs.

    • mpegaudio (supports layer I and II)

      This is the more stable MP1 and MP2 encoder.

You can read more on AMIGA audio-related stuff on the comp.sys.amiga.audio newsgroup.
All these files are linked directly to Aminet's main site at ftp.wustl.edu.
