Machine Head : The More Things Change...





I have bought this album some months ago in the first days it was out. I know this test may be a little outdated but this album needed to figure in Point & Rock.
To say it straight, "The More Things Change" is fit to be the successor of "Burn My Eyes".

This new CD begins in a fury with "Ten Ton Hammer", a very loud song. We feel in this song that Rob Flynn is upset !!! But it's good for the music...
The general atmosphere of this album is very Heavy : you won't charm your girlfriend with this kind of music ( especially if she likes the Boys Bands style... ). Everything smells sweat and hard work. Maybe you can find some darker songs like "Down To None" with a very slow and anxious intro but it soon finishes in an aggressive way.

If you have the chance to find again the digipack version of the album, you will be gratified with 3 bonus tracks with a reprise from Ice-T "Colors" and a reprise of Discharge "The Possibility Of Life Destruction". This one is quite chaotic and ultra speed but it's not to disappoint me. Colors is a rap song, quite good but it has not impressed me.

Visually, the digipack is really aggressive with dirty pics of war horrors. It's very dark and impressive...

My quote for this album is 8/10.
